Interest in I.T?

What is your interest in IT?

From here my passion for technology only went up and I decided to pursue a career in this field. Initially I began to try programming, learning languages such as Java, Python and C++. Although it was interesting and fun at times, I soon discovered programming was not for me. Then, I was introduced to the world of Cybersecurity. This field has everything I was interested in. It allows me to delve in deeper to the ethical and social aspects of technology usage. Furthermore, I can research and analyse the risks involved in technology. Lastly, it also includes great technical skills such as Linux, Bash, & terminal administration, penetration testing and network packet analysing. Who knows what the future holds, but I am currently very motivated to continue the path I am on.

Why did you choose to come to RMIT?

RMIT was a university I had in my mind when choosing where to obtain a Bachelor's degree in I.T. A few of my friends had attended RMIT and had great things to say about the city campus' facilities and overall university. In addition, RMIT is top 150 in the world for their Computer Science and Information Systems department. Therefore, it was very ideal to study Information Tech at RMIT.

What do you expect to learn during your studies?

During my studies at RMIT, I expect to learn core ethical and professional computer practices which will benefit me in my career as a I transition to full time work. Furthermore, I wish to learn and develop technical skills in my areas of study. For example; programming, security testing and cloud computing. Lastly, I expect to learn soft skills such as; to be diligent, motivated and organised, which I can translate into my everyday life.
